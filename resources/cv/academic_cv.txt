Research Projects 
SCCM: Interpretable Transfer Learning Model for Skin Cancer Classification 
(2024–2025) 
• Designed and evaluated CNN-based transfer learning architectures for 
dermoscopic image classification. 
• Fine-tuned pretrained backbones and assessed performance using internal and 
external validation datasets. 
• Applied Grad-CAM++ for explainable AI–based clinical interpretation and 
systematic error analysis. 
• Achieved 90.91% test accuracy and 93.73% external validation accuracy. 
• Published findings in a peer-reviewed journal. 
Multimodal sEMG-IMU Based Gesture Recognition (2024–2025) 
• Developed hybrid deep learning architectures for gesture recognition using surface 
electromyography (sEMG) and Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs) signals. 
• Explored signal preprocessing and representation learning for time-series 
biomedical data. 
• Performed cross-dataset evaluation to analyze robustness and scalability. 
• Contributed to feature engineering, model training, and experimental result 
analysis. 
• Investigated implications for real-time human-computer interaction using wearable 
sensor data. 
• Published findings in peer-reviewed conference papers.
